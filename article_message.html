A Message from the Editor-in-Chief
I am personally extremely excited about this new online Case Report journal, AACE Clinical Case Reports (ACCR). The decision to split off the case reports from Endocrine Practice and create a separate journal for case reports arose from discussions by the Board members of the American Association of Clinical Endocrinologists (AACE)....

It was widely believed that a separate journal will give more exposure to the case reports that otherwise were not receiving sufficient attention as part of the parent journal. Case reports have much to offer the practicing endocrinologist and allow fellows-in-training and young clinicians to expose the community to these new and interesting cases.

The format of the journal will include many more commentaries related to select case reports than before, and these will emphasize unique aspects of the selected case reports.

It is our hope that our colleagues will continue to support the journal by submissions of their cases. Initially the case report titles and abstracts will be listed in front matter of the parent journal to increase exposure of the new journal until ACCR becomes a “household” entity.

Submissions for ACCR are now being accepted. Authors are encouraged to submit their work may do so by going to https://www.aace.com/publications, where a link to submit manuscripts is available. Case reports should consist of unusual case(s) and a brief review of the literature pertaining to these case(s) that add new information to the endocrine literature. These case reports should be restricted to 800 words, 20 references, and 1 figure or table (total). Cover letter, title page and abstract are required. For questions regarding submissions to ACCR, please contact Ivette Trujillo-Mordetzki, Editorial Assistant, at endocrpractice@gmail.com.
